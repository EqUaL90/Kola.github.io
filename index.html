About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher focused on improving the resilience of water infrastructure under the impact of climate change. My work integrates AI, hydrodynamic models, smart water management techniques, reservoir modeling, and remote sensing to predict and mitigate flood and drought risks, optimize dam operations, and enhance disaster preparedness.

With expertise in hydroinformatics, deep learning, hydrologic and hydrodynamic modeling, and climate risk assessment, I execute water resources and disaster prevention projects through data-driven modeling, physics-driven AI and real-world water management practices.

Research Areas

Flood and Dam Resilience

Modeling and assessing the structural and operational resilience of dams under extreme climate events.

AI for Disaster Prediction

Harnessing deep learning and ensemble models for flood forecasting and early warning systems.

Climate Impact Modeling

Simulating hydrological systems under climate change scenarios using AI and integrated modeling frameworks.
