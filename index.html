About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher focused on improving the resilience of water infrastructure under climate change. My work integrates AI, hydrodynamic models, smart water systems, reservoir modeling, and remote sensing to predict and mitigate flood and drought risks while strengthening dam safety and disaster preparedness.

My expertise spans hydroinformatics, deep learning, hydrologic modeling, hydrodynamic simulation, and climate risk assessment. I combine data-driven approaches with physics-based models to support sustainable water management and disaster prevention.

Research Areas

Flood and Drought Risk

Hydro-climatic modeling and risk assessment of floods and droughts under changing climate conditions.

Dam Safety and Reservoir Resilience

Structural and operational assessment of dams using climate projections and reservoir hydrodynamic modeling.

AI for Disaster Prevention

Machine learning and deep learning approaches for early warning systems and predictive disaster analytics.

Climate Extremes and Hydro-Environmental Impact

Assessment of climate extremes and their hydrological and environmental consequences.

AI-IoT Smart Water Management

Integration of artificial intelligence, sensor networks, and IoT technologies for smart water infrastructure.

Biosystems Engineering

Sustainable agricultural water management, soil-water conservation, and environmental biosystems design.
