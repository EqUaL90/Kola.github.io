About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher focused on improving the resilience of water infrastructure under the impact of climate change. My work integrates AI, hydrodynamic models, and remote sensing to predict and prevent flood risks, optimize dam operations, and enhance disaster preparedness.

With expertise in hydroinformatics, machine learning, and climate risk assessment, I aim to bridge the gap between data-driven modeling and real-world water management.

Research Areas

Flood and Dam Resilience

Modeling and assessing the structural and operational resilience of dams under extreme climate events.

AI for Disaster Prediction

Leveraging deep learning and ensemble models for flood forecasting and early warning systems.

Climate Impact Modeling

Simulating hydrological systems under climate change scenarios using integrated modeling frameworks.
